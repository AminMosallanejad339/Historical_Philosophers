# Charles Darwin

## Overview

English naturalist who developed theory of evolution by natural selection, revolutionizing biology and transforming understanding of life's diversity.

## Key Information

- **Era:** 19th-Century Philosophy/Science
- **Key Concepts:** Natural Selection, Evolution, Common Descent, Adaptation
- **Major Works:** *On the Origin of Species*, *The Descent of Man*
- **Influences:** Lyell, Malthus, Humboldt
- **Legacy:** Founder of evolutionary biology; challenged creationist views

## Biography

Born February 12, 1809 in Shrewsbury; studied medicine and theology; HMS Beagle voyage 1831-1836; developed theory for decades; died April 19, 1882 at Down House.

## Philosophical Contributions

Provided naturalistic explanation for life's diversity; influenced philosophy of science and ethics; raised questions about human nature and morality.

## Major Works

1. *On the Origin of Species* (1859)
2. *The Descent of Man* (1871)
3. *The Voyage of the Beagle* (1839)

## Influence and Legacy

Revolutionized biology; influenced social thought; continues to shape scientific debates; subject of cultural controversies.
